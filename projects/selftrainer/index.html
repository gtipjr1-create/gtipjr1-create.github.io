Case study / SelfTrainer

SelfTrainer

Native rebuild in progress

A training system built to protect the quality of the work while the work is happening.

SelfTrainer puts execution before observation: see what is next, train with intent, record what actually happened, and leave with a history worth trusting.

01

The problem

The gym floor is an execution environment. The product should reduce the distance between a lifter, the planned work, and the next honest action—not add another dashboard to manage.

Working copy for WEB7 refinement: sharpen the specific tension between planning, logging, and acting without adding unsupported claims.

02

Who it is for

Serious independent lifters who already train with intent and need a calmer system for executing the plan, making honest adjustments, and preserving the record.

03

Core gym-floor workflow

  1. Up NextSurface the intended session without competing priorities.
  2. Start or ResumeEnter the work cleanly or return to a saved draft.
  3. Execute planned setsKeep the immediate prescription and logging action in view.
  4. Adjust honestlyRecord what changed without rewriting what was planned.
  5. FinishClose the active session deliberately.
  6. Preserve trustworthy historyKeep the completed record stable for later review.

06

Design and engineering decisions

Execution-first hierarchy

The next useful action carries more visual weight than surrounding context.

Planned versus logged facts

Intent and outcome stay separate so the record does not erase the plan.

Immutable completed-session history

Once a session is finished, its record is treated as history rather than a living draft.

Draft persistence and safe resume

Interrupted work should remain recoverable without pretending it was completed.

Honest unavailable states

Missing or unsupported actions are stated plainly instead of being hidden behind decorative controls.

Restrained native visual identity

Platform fit comes through hierarchy, touch, spacing, and behavior—not ornamental novelty.
